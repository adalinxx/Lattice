## Feeds: 51%-attack-cost / security-budget model C5)

Two distinct thresholds fall out of the curves and must be fed to C5 separately — conflating them silently over-states the security budget:

- **Majority threshold (safety/liveness — deep reorg & balancing): f > 50%.** Out-working a `24`-deep honest segment or stalling two equal-work branches both require a strict hashrate majority; below 50% reorg probability and balancing survival stay negligible.
- **Economic threshold (selfish-mining profitability): f = 1/3 ≈ 33.3%.** This is the *lower* economic-security bound and the binding one for the security budget. It is the analytic Eyal–Sirer crossing R(f, γ=0) = f at the γ = 0 the fork choice measures — well below majority — so a rational attacker has a revenue incentive to deviate at the classic threshold long before it can reorg or stall the chain.

C5 must price the security budget against the **lower** of the two — the selfish-mining economic threshold — not the 50% majority point. The budget is the per-chain honest hashrate cost to deny an attacker that economically-profitable fraction of that chain's subscribed PoW — independent per chain under, with no cross-chain (nexus) amplification.
